Research Contributions

Work at UNM

My Ph.D. research has been focused on two separate but related research topics. The majority of my time has been spent working towards the development of a new low-pressure gaseaous negative ion (NI) time projection chamber (TPC). Most of my work towards this development has been studying the drift properties of many different gas mixtures. I studied mixtures of either SF6 or CS2 with Xe, He, Ar and CF4, which are the candidate gases for the novel detector being developed.

Low-pressure gaseous NI TPCs are ideal for particle tracking and directional experiments because particle tracks experience the minimal diffusion, often 2-3 orders of magnitude lower than expected from the noble gases. NI gases such as SF6 and CS2 are excellent candidate because they capture ionisation quickly but still allow for stripping and amplification, given a high enough field. However, most rare searches that rely on particle tracking and directionality are more interested in interactions with noble nuclei such as Ar or Xe. Thus, the goal of my studies was to characterize mixtures between the NI and noble components, maximizing the noble gas.
